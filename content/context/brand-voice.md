# SDub Media — Brand Voice

## Who We Are
SDub Media is a Nashville video production company. We do recurring video content for businesses and a limited number of weddings each year.

## Tone
- Confident but not arrogant
- Practical — we explain what we do and why it works, no buzzwords
- We write like we talk — conversational, direct
- Nashville-rooted — we know this city and its businesses

## What We Don't Say
- No "leverage," "utilize," "delve," "game-changing," "unlock," "elevate"
- No corporate-speak
- No fluff. Every sentence earns its place.
- NO EM-DASHES (—). Use a colon and a space, or a period, or commas. See the style guide.

## Writing Style
- Short paragraphs (2-4 sentences max)
- Varied sentence lengths — mix short punchy lines with longer ones
- No four-bullet-point patterns every time — vary the structure
- Lead with the point, not the buildup
- Blog posts: minimum 1,500 words
- Pillar pages: minimum 2,500 words

## Our Clients — REAL LIST ONLY (hard rule)
NEVER invent a client, a client story, a quote, an industry we serve, or a location. No fabrication, ever. This is a trust and honesty rule with no exceptions.

The ONLY clients you may name (all real):

Tone Films and Lighthouse Media appear under "trusted by" — they are peers, NOT clients. Do not claim them.

"An Alabama home builder" may be referenced in BLOG POSTS only (never on website pages), and only as a fallback after you've used the named clients first.

When you need a concrete example: use a real client from the list (truthfully — don't invent what was filmed for them), or frame it as openly hypothetical ("say a dance studio wants to show off a recital..."). Never present an invented specific as something that actually happened. Never invent a customer quote.
