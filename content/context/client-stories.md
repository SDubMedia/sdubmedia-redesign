# Client Stories — Real Examples for Blog Posts

TRUE stories from real SDub Media work. Everything here is real and provided by Geoff.

## How often to use a story (important)
Stories are a spice, not a staple. **Most articles need NO specific client story** — write from general expertise. Use a real story from this file only when it genuinely strengthens a point, and sparingly: aim for at most about **one article in three** to feature a specific client. Never force one. A clean, principle-based article with no named client is the norm and perfectly fine. If a point needs an example and no real story fits, make it openly hypothetical ("say a dance studio wants...") and NEVER invent a real-sounding client, quote, or event.

## Naming rules
- **OK to name:** Coldwell Banker Southern Realty, Webb School, Elite Dance, Nolensville Dance Company, Tutoring Rocks, Middle of the Story Podcast.
- **Keep generic** (true but unnamed): e.g., "an Alabama home builder", "a Nashville dance studio". Never invent a name or location for these.
- Tone Films and Lighthouse Media are peers, not clients. Never claim them.

## Format for each story
```
### Short title
- Use it for: which topics/clusters this fits (corporate, testimonial, event, dance, school, real estate, podcast, etc.)
- Naming: OK to name [client] / keep generic
- Story: what they needed, how the video came about, what we did, the outcome or the lesson
```

### Ballet Ball Nashville — live event video
- Use it for: event video / event coverage (gala, live performance), corporate-video cluster
- Naming: OK to name (Geoff confirmed 2026-05-25).
- Story: Hired to produce a live event video for Ballet Ball Nashville. Had full backstage access. Filmed the ballet dancers performing on stage from multiple angles, and captured the whole feel of the night: the entrance to the venue, plenty of detail shots, guests dancing, people having fun, cocktails. The brief was to capture both the performance and the atmosphere of a live event in a single recap. A strong example of event coverage that blends a main performance with the energy and details around it.

### Tutoring Rocks — brand / commercial video (Nolensville)
- Use it for: brand/commercial video, "who we are" films, voiceover and audio production tips; video-production and corporate-video clusters
- Naming: OK to name (Tutoring Rocks is an approved client, on the website).
- Story: Hired to tag along with the photographer and produce a commercial/brand video highlighting who Tutoring Rocks is, for their Nolensville, Tennessee location. Filmed kids and tutors inside the building and a coffee shop, shot outside the coffee shop, and captured a giant "Nolensville" sign for local flavor. For the voiceover, pulled copy straight from their website and had one of their supervised employees read the lines. Recorded that voiceover in a closet to get cleaner sound, since the available mic needed a quiet, soft space. It turned out great and is featured on SDub Media's Instagram.
- Production tip embedded here: recording voiceover in a closet (clothes absorb echo) is a real trick for clean audio when you don't have a treated room.

- Why it matters: this is recurring content for one client across many formats (series, headshots, podcast, events), not a one-off shoot. It is the single best real example for any post about consistency, retainers, or "showing up every week."
